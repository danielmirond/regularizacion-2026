Overview

The 2026 extraordinary regularization is a process by the Spanish Government allowing migrants already in Spain to obtain a residence and work permit. Applications are filed through the Mercurio platform, require specific documentation (passport, padrón registration, criminal record, and often a vulnerability certificate), and involve paying the 790-052 fee. Non-profit organizations can help you free of charge at every step.
