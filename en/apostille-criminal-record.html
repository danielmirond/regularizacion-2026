Hague Apostille

What is it?

The apostille is an international seal that validates a public document for use in another country party to the 1961 Hague Convention. It replaces the traditional diplomatic legalization.

Who issues it

  • Issued by the country where the document originated, not Spain.
  • Usually by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or Supreme Court.
  • Spain only apostilles Spanish documents going abroad.

If your country is not party

You need consular legalization: MFA of origin country + Spanish Consulate there. Slower (months).

Electronic apostille

Many countries now issue e-apostille (PDF with QR code and digital signature). Spain accepts e-apostille since 2024.
