What is Mercurio?

Mercurio is Spain's official telematic platform run by the Ministry of Territorial Policy and the Ministry of Inclusion. From it you can:

  • File residence and work permit applications.
  • Book appointments for in-person procedures.
  • Upload additional documents to a file.
  • Check the status of your case.
  • Receive official notifications via DEHÚ.

Access requirements

  • Digital certificate installed in your browser (FNMT, Camerfirma or similar).
  • Or DNIe (Spanish e-ID) with a compatible reader.
  • Or Cl@ve system (PIN or permanent).
  • Updated browser (Chrome, Firefox or Edge).

If you don't have a digital certificate, get it free at FNMT. If your NIE is still pending, you can use Cl@ve with your passport number.

Step-by-step

  1. Go to Mercurio on the official site.
  2. Log in with digital certificate, DNIe or Cl@ve.
  3. Select your province.
  4. Choose the procedure (initial application, document upload, appointment, case status).
  5. Complete the form and attach the required PDFs.
  6. Sign digitally and submit. Keep the submission receipt.
  7. Check DEHÚ regularly for notifications.

Appointment booking

Slots are released continuously. If none available, retry at different hours (late night and early morning work well). Keep your confirmation email and arrive 10 minutes early.

Go to appointment portal (ICP)

File status

Common status values:

  • In process, being reviewed.
  • Requirement, additional documents needed. Check DEHÚ.
  • Favorable resolution, permit granted.
  • Denied, you can appeal within the legal deadlines.

DEHÚ notifications

DEHÚ (dehu.redsara.es) is where official notifications are delivered. Not opening them within 10 calendar days = treated as rejected. Enable email/SMS alerts.

FAQ

Can I use Mercurio without a digital certificate?

Not for authenticated procedures. Cl@ve is free and obtainable online or at registration offices.

No appointments available, what now?

Slots are released continuously; retry at different hours. Some NGOs (CEAR, Red Cross) can help with urgent cases.

What does "regularización masiva" mean?

It's the colloquial name for the 2026 extraordinary regularization procedure.

How do I sign the PDFs I upload?

Use Autofirma (free, from the Government) or your browser's digital certificate.
